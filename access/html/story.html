SOLITUDE COFFEE ADVENTURE

Coffee (from the French: café [/kafe/])[ is a drink brewed from roasted coffee beans, obtained from the fruit of the coffee plant. Coffee cultivars are native to tropical Africa and parts of Madagascar, Comoros, Mauritius and Réunion on equatorial areas. It is exported from Africa to countries around the world and is now cultivated in a total of more than 70 countries, mainly near the equator in the Americas, Southeast Asia, India and Africa. The two most commonly cultivated coffee varieties are tea coffee, and tap coffee. Once ripe, coffee berries will be picked, processed and dried. Dried coffee beans will be roasted in a variety of temperature conditions, depending on the needs of tastes. After roasting, coffee beans will be ground and brewed with boiling water to create coffee in the form of a drink. Coffee is less acidic and can be irritating to users due to its caffeine content. Coffee today is one of the most popular drinks in the world. It can be prepared and served in a variety of drink formats (e.g. espresso, pitched coffee, latte,...). Coffee is usually enjoyed hot, although iced coffee is also preferred by many people. Many clinical studies show that the average amount of coffee consumed is sufficient or beneficial for a healthy adult. Many researchers also question whether long-term coffee consumption can limit senile memory decline or minimize the likelihood of cancer. The earliest and most reliable evidence of coffee use was discovered in the 15th century at Sufi tombs in Yemen. Also in the Arabian Peninsula, the first coffee beans were roasted and brewed in a manner similar to the method we still do today. The beans were originally exported from East Africa to Yemen, as the tea plant at the time was believed to have originated from indigenous people. Yemeni traders brought coffee home and began planting the seeds. By the 16th century, coffee was being brought to Persia, Turkey and North Africa. From here, coffee was spread throughout Europe and the rest of the world.
